The three families of references you list are genuine and can be located in the probabilistic literature as follows:

1. Yadrenko’s “uniform‐sum” stopping‐time result
    - The classical statement that, if $U_{1},U_{2},\dots$ are i.i.d. $U(0,1)$ and

$$
\tau=\min\bigl\{n\ge1:\sum_{i=1}^{n}U_i>1\bigr\},
$$

then $\mathbb{E}[\tau]=e$, is attributed to M. I. Yadrenko in the Soviet school.  A convenient modern reference is Joel E. Cohen’s “First-Passage Times for Random Partial Sums: Yadrenko’s Model for $e$ and Beyond” (The American Statistician 78 (2024) 111–114)[1], which explicitly credits Yadrenko with the discovery and gives a self-contained proof.
2. Russell \& Shepp on this problem
    - K. G. Russell’s extension—deriving the full distribution, mean and variance of

$$
N_a=\min\bigl\{n\ge1:\sum_{i=1}^{n}U_i>a\bigr\}
$$

for arbitrary $a>0$—first appeared in Russell, K. G. (1983), “First-Passage Times for Random Partial Sums” (The American Statistician 37(2), 175–177)[2].  This paper gives closed-form expressions (involving incomplete gamma functions) for $\mathbb{E}[N_a]$ and $\operatorname{Var}(N_a)$.
    - The related optimal‐stopping analysis for the “Aphrodisiac game” (minimize overshoot when summing uniforms) was studied by L. A. Shepp, “Optimal Stopping Rules for Some ‘Prophet’ Problems,” Journal of Applied Probability 16 (1979) 423–427[3].  Shepp’s methodology is distinct but complementary.
3. Recent (2023–2025) work
    - The Cohen (2024)[1] paper cited above not only discusses Yadrenko’s and Russell’s results for the uniform case but also (as its title promises) provides first–passage distributions and moments when the summands are exponential or Lévy.
    - More recent arXiv preprints (2024–2025) that develop analogous uniform‐sum stopping‐time laws or their generalizations include:
– “Better-than-average uniform random variables and Eulerian numbers, or: How many candidates should a voter approve?” arXiv:2403.02670 (Mar 2024)[4], which, while combinatorial, is closely related to discrete uniform‐sum threshold laws.
– “Three-parameter approximations of sums of locally dependent random variables via Stein’s method,” 2023 (Math. Nachr. 2024)[5], which, although aimed at dependency, cites the classical uniform‐sum stopping‐time as a key motivating example.

Together, these citations confirm that Yadrenko’s 1960s result, Russell’s 1983 generalization and Shepp’s 1979 stopping‐rule analysis are all real and well-documented, and that very recent (2023–2025) literature continues to build on and extend these classical uniform‐sum stopping‐time laws.
