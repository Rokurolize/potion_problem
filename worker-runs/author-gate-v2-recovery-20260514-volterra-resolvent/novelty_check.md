# Novelty check

Compared with:

- AntiderivativeVolumeSolution.md: does not focus on primitive recursion of volumes.
- FundamentalTheoremTailSolution.md: does not use FTC on the tail sequence as the main object.
- LeibnizIntegralRuleSolution.md: no parameterized tail integral differentiation.
- CompactOperatorSimplexSolution.md: avoids compactness language and states the concrete killed operator.
- KilledExponentialSolution.md: shares killed-process spirit but frames the answer as a unit-cost resolvent.
- StieltjesMomentTailSolution.md: not a moment problem.
- BanachNormTailSolution.md: not a norm convergence argument.
- DynamicProgrammingTailSolution.md: not a finite-horizon recursion.
- HittingTimeVolumeSolution.md: does not enumerate stopped volumes.
- CauchyProductPrefixSolution.md: not a product or coefficient extraction.

This is not merely a rename because the proof object is the operator
`(I-K)^{-1}1` and its killed-state interpretation, with the tail formula
appearing only as the expansion of that resolvent.
