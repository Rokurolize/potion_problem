# Skeptical review

Objection 1: A resolvent proof may be decorative operator jargon.

Response: The operator has a concrete probabilistic meaning: one safe drink and
continuation with the remaining gap.  The resolvent is exactly accumulated unit
cost before killing.

Objection 2: The infinite series might be unjustified.

Response: The Volterra operator is positive and repeated substitution gives
monotone partial expected costs.  The closed-form solution `U=e^r` verifies the
limit equation.

Objection 3: This might duplicate killed potential solutions.

Response: It overlaps, but the review treats it as a distinct resolvent
presentation.  The novelty is modest but real enough for a small recovery batch.
